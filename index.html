Discovery Flights

A discovery flight is the best way to experience what flying is really like. You’ll begin with a structured ground briefing covering the basics of flight, the aircraft, and what to expect — then take the controls yourself during a one‑hour introductory flight from Eastern WV Regional Airport (KMRB).

Flights are conducted in a well‑maintained Piper PA‑28 and are open to anyone with no prior experience required. The $200 flat rate includes both ground instruction and flight time. Weekend availability fills quickly, so early scheduling is recommended.

Training Aircraft

Training is conducted in a well‑maintained Piper PA‑28, a stable and predictable platform trusted by flight schools for decades. Its handling characteristics, visibility, and forgiving flight envelope make it ideal for both new pilots and advanced training.

The aircraft is IFR‑certified, ADS‑B equipped, and maintained with a long‑term ownership mindset — emphasizing reliability, documentation, and consistent operating practices. Students benefit from a clean, professionally managed training environment that supports efficient learning at Eastern WV Regional Airport (KMRB).

Whether you're beginning your aviation journey or advancing toward instrument or commercial training, the PA‑28 provides a safe, capable, and confidence‑building platform for every stage.

Training Location

Flight training is conducted at Eastern West Virginia Regional Airport (KMRB), where the aircraft is hangared and all instruction originates. KMRB offers a towered environment, long runways, low congestion, and excellent IFR procedures — ideal for efficient, real‑world training.

Central Aviation serves students from Martinsburg WV, Winchester VA, Hagerstown MD, Charles Town WV, and Northern Virginia. Many students choose KMRB for its professional training environment without the delays and congestion common at larger Northern Virginia airports.

Principal Instructor & Operator

Central Aviation is led by a certificated flight instructor with experience in both general aviation operations and formal regulatory environments. Prior to flight instruction, the operator worked in public‑sector safety and compliance roles, developing a disciplined approach to risk management and operational oversight.

This background directly shapes the training environment at Central Aviation: structured lessons, clear expectations, and a focus on building pilots who can operate confidently and safely in the National Airspace System — not just pass a checkride.

Students describe the instructional style as calm, methodical, and supportive, with an emphasis on fundamentals, sound judgment, and long‑term skill development. Whether you're pursuing aviation professionally or flying for personal enjoyment, the goal is to help you build the competence and confidence to fly well beyond the minimums.

Is Central Aviation a Good Fit?

Central Aviation is best suited for students who value structure, professionalism, and long‑term skill development. The training environment at KMRB emphasizes disciplined fundamentals, consistent progression, and real‑world readiness.

A good fit if you:

  • Prefer disciplined, syllabus‑based instruction
  • Can train consistently and value steady progression
  • Prioritize safety, preparation, and sound judgment
  • Want a professional training environment at a towered airport
  • Are pursuing aviation seriously — career or personal mastery

May not be a fit if you:

  • Are seeking the lowest possible hourly rate
  • Prefer informal or unstructured instruction
  • Can only train sporadically or without consistency
  • Are looking for a casual “club” environment
