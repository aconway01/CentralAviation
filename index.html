Discovery Flights

Not sure if flying is right for you? A discovery flight is the best way to find out. You'll start with a structured ground briefing covering the basics of flight, the aircraft, and what to expect in the air — then take the controls yourself on a one-hour introductory flight.

Discovery flights are conducted in the Piper PA-28 and are open to anyone with no prior experience required. The $200 flat rate covers the full hour including ground time.

Training Investment & Planning Guidance

Training is conducted on an hourly basis and tailored to each student’s experience, schedule, and objectives. The ranges below are provided for planning purposes.

Career / Professional Track

Students pursuing aviation professionally should plan for consistent training and structured progression.

  • Private Pilot: typically $14,000–$18,000
  • Instrument Rating: typically $9,000–$12,000
  • Ongoing training discounted through continuity

Recreational / Personal Flying

Flexible scheduling is available, with costs varying based on training frequency and goals.

  • Pay-as-you-go instruction available
  • Clear guidance provided before training begins

Detailed estimates are provided after an initial consultation.

Training Aircraft

Training is conducted in a well-maintained Piper PA-28 platform, selected for its predictable handling characteristics, instructional suitability, and strong safety record.

Aircraft maintenance, documentation, and operating practices are managed with a long-term ownership mindset and a focus on reliability and consistency.

What to Expect as a Student

  • Structured lesson plans aligned with FAA ACS standards
  • Professional scheduling and clear expectations
  • Emphasis on aeronautical decision-making and risk management
  • Training records maintained to professional and insurance standards

Training Location

Flight training is conducted at Eastern West Virginia Regional Airport (KMRB), where the aircraft is hangared and instruction is based.

Central Aviation is administratively based in Sterling, Virginia, and serves students from Northern Virginia, the Eastern Panhandle of West Virginia, and the surrounding region.

Principal Instructor & Operator

Central Aviation is led by a certificated flight instructor with experience in both general aviation operations and formal regulatory environments. Prior to flight instruction, the operator worked in public-sector regulatory oversight roles involving safety, compliance, and risk management in highly scrutinized industries.

As an instructor, the focus is on disciplined fundamentals, structured progression, and preparing pilots to operate safely and confidently in the National Airspace System — not just to pass a checkride.

Is Central Aviation a Good Fit?

Central Aviation is best suited for students who value structured training, professionalism, and long-term skill development.

A good fit if you:

  • Want disciplined, syllabus-based instruction
  • Can train consistently
  • Value safety and preparation over shortcuts
  • Are considering aviation as a serious pursuit

May not be a fit if you:

  • Are seeking the lowest possible hourly rate
  • Prefer informal or unstructured instruction
  • Can only train sporadically
